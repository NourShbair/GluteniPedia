What is Gluten?

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and related grains. It’s what gives dough its elasticity, allowing it to rise and maintain its shape, and gives bread its chewy texture. While gluten is a common component in many foods, it’s important to know that some people may have sensitivities or conditions like celiac disease that require them to avoid it. Whether you’re looking to learn more about gluten, manage a gluten-free diet, or simply explore gluten’s role in your food, our site offers valuable insights and resources to help you make informed choices.

Red Flag Symptoms

Celiac disease and gluten intolerance can manifest through a variety of symptoms, affecting both the digestive system and other parts of the body. Common symptoms include bloating, diarrhea, constipation, and abdominal pain. People with these conditions might also experience fatigue, headaches, and joint pain. In some cases, gluten can lead to skin rashes, mood changes, and nutrient deficiencies. It’s important to note that symptoms can vary widely from person to person, making it crucial to consult with a healthcare provider for an accurate diagnosis and appropriate dietary management.
